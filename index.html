Welcome to Tharlam-Academy

Where Wisdom Meets Modern Learning

Inspired by Buddhist values and supported by a modern curriculum, we offer students a well-rounded education rooted in tradition and aimed at the future.


A Unique Educational Experience

Tharlam Academy is a unique institution where modern academic learning meets the rich traditions of Buddhist philosophy and culture. Founded with the vision of nurturing compassionate, well-rounded individuals....

Our Mission Statement

At Tharlam Academy, we provide a holistic education grounded in Buddhist philosophy and modern knowledge. Our mission is to nurture the next generation of bodhisattvas—leaders dedicated to compassion, wisdom, and service to all sentient beings.

Curriculum Overview

Combining traditional Buddhist practices with modern academic subjects, our curriculum nurtures the mind, heart, and spirit.


Pre-Primary Level (class 4–6)

At the Pre-Primary Level (Classes 4 to 6), students are introduced to basic reading and writing in the Tibetan language. They also begin learning basic English and Mathematics. The daily routine includes prayers, along with engaging in creative and physical activities to support their overall development.

Introduction of Tharlam Academy


the academy provides a balanced curriculum that integrates formal education with moral and spiritual development.


Our Students and Their Daily Practices

With around 100 students, Tharlam Academy creates a close-knit community that fosters both academic and personal growth. Our students engage in daily practices designed to nurture their intellectual, physical, and spiritual development.

Each day begins with meditation and mindfulness sessions, which help students cultivate focus, discipline, and emotional well-being. In addition to academic lessons, students participate in a variety of cultural activities, physical education, and spiritual practices, allowing them to grow as balanced individuals. This holistic approach encourages students to develop a deep sense of responsibility, empathy, and respect for others.


Encouraging Exploration and Growth

At Tharlam Academy, we encourage students to explore both intellectual and creative pursuits. From engaging classroom lessons to cultural activities and house competitions, we create an environment that promotes curiosity, cooperation, and self-expression. The academy emphasizes values such as kindness, discipline, and respect—core principles of Buddhist heritage that inspire our teaching philosophy.


Beyond Academics: Engaging with the Community

Tharlam Academy is deeply committed to community involvement. We organize health checkups, cultural excursions, and outreach programs that connect students with their local environment. These activities not only enhance learning but also foster a sense of social responsibility. With the support of dedicated donors and educators, the school continues to grow as a center for holistic education, preparing students not just for exams but for life.


Learning for Life

At Tharlam Academy, learning extends beyond textbooks. Our classrooms are spaces for discovery, creativity, and personal growth. Through cultural events, academic challenges, and community engagement, students learn not only how to succeed but also how to make a meaningful difference in the world.


Our Commitment to the Future

Guided by dedicated educators and supported by our wider community, we are committed to nurturing thoughtful, responsible individuals who are ready to contribute to a better world.

Tharlam-Academhy Curriculum


Primary Level (Class 1–5)

At the Primary Level (Classes 1 to 5), students study Tibetan and English languages, along with Mathematics, Science, and Social Studies. They are also introduced to Buddhist Studies and basic ritual practices as part of their cultural and spiritual education.


Secondary Level (Class 6–8)

At the Secondary Level (Classes 6 to 8), students delve into advanced Tibetan grammar, English composition, and continue their studies in Math and Science. Buddhist Studies cover key teachings such as the Four Noble Truths and the Eightfold Path. Ritual training includes practices like making torma, performing pujas, and chanting sutras.


Special Buddhist Practices

In addition to academic and ritual studies, students also learn and practice traditional spiritual activities such as Mandala Offering, Prostrations, and Guru Yoga. They actively participate in important Buddha event celebrations, deepening their connection to Buddhist culture and teachings.
